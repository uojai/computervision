McKinsey Ecosystem Building Drills

Practice for the McKinsey Problem Solving Game with these 5 ecosystem building exercises. Each drill has a 35-minute time limit.

Drill: 1/5
Time Limit: 35:00
Elapsed: 00:00

Drill 1: Forest Ecosystem

Terrain: Dense forest with a river running through it

Objective: Build an ecosystem with 8 species that forms a complete food chain with calorie surplus for each predator-prey pair. The ecosystem must be suited to the forest terrain.

Available Species (Select 8):

Your Ecosystem:

Recommended Solution:

Food Chain: Oak Tree → Caterpillar → Songbird → Hawk

Additional Species: Deer, Fox, Mushrooms, Earthworm

Calorie Analysis:

  • Oak Tree (50,000 cal) supports many caterpillars (each needs 200 cal)
  • Each songbird needs 5 caterpillars (1,000 cal total)
  • Hawk needs 3 songbirds (3,000 cal)

Terrain Fit: All species are native to forest environments. The river provides water for all species.

Key Considerations:

  1. Start with a primary producer (Oak Tree)
  2. Add herbivores that feed on it (Caterpillar, Deer)
  3. Add predators at different levels (Songbird, Fox, Hawk)
  4. Include decomposers (Mushrooms, Earthworm) to complete nutrient cycle
  5. Verify calorie needs are met at each level

Drill 2: Coral Reef Ecosystem

Terrain: Tropical coral reef in shallow waters

Objective: Build an ecosystem with 8 species that forms a complete food chain with calorie surplus for each predator-prey pair. The ecosystem must be suited to coral reef environments.

Available Species (Select 8):

Your Ecosystem:

Recommended Solution:

Food Chain: Phytoplankton → Zooplankton → Clownfish → Moray Eel

Additional Species: Coral, Sea Turtle, Parrotfish, Cleaner Shrimp

Calorie Analysis:

  • Phytoplankton (massive caloric production) supports zooplankton
  • Zooplankton colonies support small fish populations
  • Clownfish need about 50 zooplankton per day (500 cal)
  • Moray eel needs about 3 clownfish per week (3,000 cal)

Terrain Fit: All species are native to coral reef ecosystems. Coral provides shelter for many species.

Key Considerations:

  1. Start with microscopic producers (Phytoplankton)
  2. Add filter feeders (Zooplankton, Coral)
  3. Include small fish (Clownfish) and their predators (Moray Eel)
  4. Add specialized species (Cleaner Shrimp) for ecosystem balance
  5. Include herbivores (Parrotfish) to prevent algal overgrowth

Drill 3: Grassland Ecosystem

Terrain: Temperate grassland with seasonal rainfall

Objective: Build an ecosystem with 8 species that forms a complete food chain with calorie surplus for each predator-prey pair. The ecosystem must be suited to grassland environments.

Available Species (Select 8):

Your Ecosystem:

Recommended Solution:

Food Chain: Grass → Grasshopper → Prairie Dog → Coyote

Additional Species: Bison, Hawk, Earthworm, Wildflower

Calorie Analysis:

  • 1 acre of grass produces ~2 million calories annually
  • Grasshoppers consume about 1/2 their weight daily (50 cal)
  • Prairie dogs need ~100 grasshoppers daily (5,000 cal)
  • Coyote needs about 1 prairie dog every 3 days (1,600 cal/day)

Terrain Fit: All species are adapted to open grassland environments with seasonal variations.

Key Considerations:

  1. Include primary grazers (Bison) to maintain grass health
  2. Add multiple predator levels to balance populations
  3. Include pollinators (bees via wildflowers) for plant reproduction
  4. Add decomposers (Earthworm) for nutrient cycling
  5. Ensure apex predator (Coyote) has sufficient prey base

Drill 4: Arctic Tundra Ecosystem

Terrain: Frozen tundra with permafrost, short summers

Objective: Build an ecosystem with 8 species that forms a complete food chain with calorie surplus for each predator-prey pair. The ecosystem must be suited to extreme cold environments.

Available Species (Select 8):

Your Ecosystem:

Recommended Solution:

Food Chain: Lichen → Arctic Hare → Arctic Fox → Polar Bear

Additional Species: Moss, Lemming, Snowy Owl, Caribou

Calorie Analysis:

  • Lichen grows slowly but is extremely nutrient-dense (500 cal/sq meter)
  • Arctic hare needs ~1kg lichen daily (3,000 cal)
  • Arctic fox needs 1 hare every 5 days (600 cal/day)
  • Polar bear needs 1 fox every 10 days (plus seals, 12,000 cal/day)

Terrain Fit: All species have adaptations for extreme cold (thick fur, fat layers, hibernation abilities).

Key Considerations:

  1. Focus on cold-adapted primary producers (Lichen, Moss)
  2. Include migratory species (Caribou) that can exploit seasonal resources
  3. Add multiple small prey options (Lemming, Hare) to support predators
  4. Account for lower productivity with higher calorie storage needs
  5. Include apex predator (Polar Bear) but note it may need additional food sources

Drill 5: Urban Ecosystem

Terrain: Dense city with parks and water features

Objective: Build an ecosystem with 8 species that forms a complete food chain with calorie surplus for each predator-prey pair. The ecosystem must be suited to urban environments.

Available Species (Select 8):

Your Ecosystem:

Recommended Solution:

Food Chain: Street Trees → Pigeon → Peregrine Falcon

Additional Species: Rats, Squirrels, Raccoons, Insects, Domestic Cats, Algae

Calorie Analysis:

  • Urban trees produce limited but concentrated calories (fruit, nuts)
  • Pigeons need ~30g seeds daily (150 cal)
  • Peregrine falcon needs 1 pigeon every 2-3 days (300 cal/day)
  • Raccoons and cats supplement with human garbage

Terrain Fit: All species are adapted to human presence and can exploit artificial structures.

Key Considerations:

  1. Include species that can utilize human resources (garbage, feeders)
  2. Account for vertical space (buildings for birds, underground for rodents)
  3. Include both natural and human-assisted food sources
  4. Consider disease vectors in dense populations
  5. Balance predator-prey ratios given artificial constraints
