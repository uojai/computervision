Terrain Settings:

  • Elevation: 2800m
  • Soil pH: 5.5
  • Precipitation: 900mm
  • Temperature: 3°C
  • Wind Speed: 40km/h
  • Air Pressure: 800hPa
  • Humidity: 70%
  • Cloud Height: 1800m

Calorie Analysis:

  • Alpine Grass: Produces 50,000 cal (no consumption)
  • Marmot: Needs 3,000 cal (consumes 5% of grass = 2,500 cal) → Warning
  • Golden Eagle: Needs 1,200 cal (consumes 1 marmot/week = ~600 cal) → Warning
  • Solution: Add more producers (Mountain Pine) and adjust terrain to support larger grass production

Key Considerations:

  1. Start with producers that match mountain terrain (Alpine Grass, Mountain Pine)
  2. Add herbivores that can survive at high elevation (Marmot, Mountain Goat)
  3. Include apex predators (Golden Eagle, Lynx) but ensure sufficient prey
  4. Adjust terrain parameters to meet most species' requirements
  5. Balance calorie needs across the food chain

Terrain Settings:

  • Depth: 12m
  • Water Current: 0.8m/s
  • Water Clarity: 15m
  • Temperature: 28°C
  • Salt Content: 34ppt
  • Dissolved Oxygen: 6.5mg/L
  • Wind Speed: 15km/h

Calorie Analysis:

  • Coral Polyps: Produce 40,000 cal (consumed by Parrotfish)
  • Parrotfish: Needs 800 cal (consumes 2% of coral = 800 cal) → Balanced
  • Moray Eel: Needs 1,200 cal (consumes 1 parrotfish/week = ~800 cal) → Warning
  • Solution: Add Zooplankton as additional food source for small fish

Key Considerations:

  1. Coral requires specific water conditions (temp, clarity, current)
  2. Include multiple food sources for predators (Moray eats both Parrotfish and Clownfish)
  3. Add symbiotic relationships (Cleaner Shrimp)
  4. Balance herbivores to prevent coral overgrazing
