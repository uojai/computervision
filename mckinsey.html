Percentages & Growth Rates

These problems test your ability to work with percentage changes, compound growth, and decay. Key concepts:

  • Basic Percentage: X% of Y = (X/100) × Y
  • Growth: New Value = Original × (1 + %)
  • Decay: New Value = Original × (1 - %)
  • Compound Growth: Value = Original × (1 + %)n (n = years)

McKinsey uses these for revenue projections, population changes, and cost adjustments.

Percentage & Growth Drills

1. A coastal town has 1,200 residents. If the population grows by 5% annually, how many residents will there be in 3 years? (Round to nearest whole number)

Solution:

Compound growth formula: Final = Initial × (1 + rate)years

1200 × (1.05)3 = 1200 × 1.157625 ≈ 1389 residents

2. A coral reef covers 500m². If it's shrinking by 8% each year due to bleaching, what area will remain after 2 years? (Round to 1 decimal place)

Solution:

Compound decay formula: Final = Initial × (1 - rate)years

500 × (0.92)2 = 500 × 0.8464 = 423.2m²

3. A fishery's revenue was $2.5M last year. If revenue grows 12% this year but then declines 5% next year, what will revenue be in 2 years? (Round to nearest $10K)

Solution:

Year 1: $2.5M × 1.12 = $2.8M

Year 2: $2.8M × 0.95 = $2.66M → $2,660,000

4. A mangrove forest has 8,000 trees. If 15% are lost to logging and 5% to disease in one year, how many trees remain? (Assume losses are sequential)

Solution:

After logging: 8000 × 0.85 = 6800 trees

After disease: 6800 × 0.95 = 6460 trees

5. A recycling program processes 1,200 tons/year. If capacity increases by 20% in Year 1 and 15% in Year 2, what will annual capacity be? (Round to nearest ton)

Solution:

Year 1: 1200 × 1.20 = 1440 tons

Year 2: 1440 × 1.15 = 1656 tons

6. A company's CO2 emissions are 50,000 tons. If they reduce emissions by 8% annually for 5 years, what will emissions be? (Round to nearest ton)

Solution:

Compound reduction: 50,000 × (0.92)5

50,000 × 0.65908 ≈ 32,954 tons

7. A wind farm produces 80MW. If output increases 25% in Phase 1 and then decreases 10% in Phase 2 due to technical issues, what's the final output? (Round to 1 decimal)

Solution:

Phase 1: 80 × 1.25 = 100MW

Phase 2: 100 × 0.90 = 90MW

8. A city's water consumption is 1.5M gallons/day. If conservation efforts reduce usage by 3% monthly (compounded), what's consumption after 6 months? (Round to nearest 10K gallons)

Solution:

1.5M × (0.97)6 = 1.5M × 0.833 ≈ 1.25M gallons

9. A startup's user base grows from 10K to 35K in 3 years. What's the approximate CAGR (Compound Annual Growth Rate)? (Round to nearest %)

Solution:

CAGR formula: (End/Start)(1/years) - 1

(35,000/10,000)1/3 - 1 ≈ 1.518 - 1 = 51.8% → 52%

10. A stock price falls 20% in Q1, rises 30% in Q2, then falls 10% in Q3. What's the net change over 3 quarters? (Round to 1 decimal)

Solution:

Assume $100 start: 100 × 0.80 = $80

80 × 1.30 = $104

104 × 0.90 = $93.60

Net change: (93.60 - 100)/100 = -6.4%

Breakeven Analysis

These problems determine the point where costs equal benefits. Key formulas:

  • Basic Breakeven: Units = Fixed Costs / (Price per Unit - Variable Cost per Unit)
  • Time-based: Breakeven Time = Investment / Annual Profit
  • With Targets: (Fixed Costs + Target Profit) / Contribution Margin

McKinsey uses this for market entry decisions, product launches, and sustainability projects.

Breakeven Drills

1. A beach cleanup machine costs $25,000. Each ton of plastic collected can be sold for $150. How many tons must be collected to break even?

Solution:

Breakeven Quantity = Fixed Costs / Revenue per Unit

$25,000 / $150 ≈ 166.67 → Round up to 167 tons

2. A solar panel installation costs $50,000 upfront and saves $8,000/year in energy costs. How many years until breakeven? (Round to 1 decimal)

Solution:

Breakeven Time = Investment / Annual Savings

$50,000 / $8,000 = 6.25 years → 6.3 years

3. A recycling plant costs $2M to build. Processing each ton costs $30, and recycled material sells for $80/ton. What's the breakeven volume?

Solution:

Contribution Margin = $80 - $30 = $50/ton

Breakeven = $2,000,000 / $50 = 40,000 tons

4. An EV charging station costs $120K to install. Each charge generates $5 profit. How many charges/day are needed to break even in 3 years?

Solution:

Total days = 3 × 365 = 1,095 days

Breakeven per day = $120,000 / (1,095 × $5) ≈ 21.9 → 22 charges/day

5. A consulting project has fixed costs of $80K, variable costs of $20K/client, and charges $45K/client. How many clients to break even?

Solution:

Contribution Margin = $45K - $20K = $25K/client

$80K / $25K = 3.2 → Round up to 4 clients

6. A factory upgrade costs $1.5M but reduces per-unit costs from $8 to $6. At 500K units/year, how many years to break even?

Solution:

Annual savings = 500K × ($8 - $6) = $1M/year

Breakeven = $1.5M / $1M = 1.5 years

7. A SaaS product costs $200K/year to maintain. Each subscription is $50/month with $10 variable cost. How many subscribers to break even?

Solution:

Monthly profit/subscriber = $50 - $10 = $40

Annual profit/subscriber = $40 × 12 = $480

$200,000 / $480 ≈ 416.67 → 417 subscribers

8. A restaurant's fixed costs are $15K/month. Average meal profit is $8. How many meals/month to earn $5K profit?

Solution:

Total needed = $15K + $5K = $20K

$20,000 / $8 = 2,500 meals

9. A wind turbine costs $3M. It generates $400K/year after operating costs. What's the breakeven time if you also get $500K in tax credits?

Solution:

Net investment = $3M - $500K = $2.5M

$2.5M / $0.4M = 6.25 → 6.3 years

10. A product costs $50 to make and sells for $120. Fixed costs are $350K. How many units to earn $210K profit?

Solution:

Contribution Margin = $120 - $50 = $70/unit

($350K + $210K) / $70 = 8,000 units

Cost-Benefit Ratios

These problems compare alternatives based on efficiency metrics. Key approaches:

  • Cost per Unit: Total Cost / Units
  • Benefit per Dollar: Total Benefit / Total Cost
  • ROI: (Benefit - Cost) / Cost

McKinsey uses this to prioritize projects, allocate resources, and optimize operations.

Cost-Benefit Drills

1. Option A cleans 100 tons for $1,000. Option B cleans 150 tons for $1,500. Which is more cost-effective?

Solution:

Option A: $1,000 / 100 = $10/ton

Option B: $1,500 / 150 = $10/ton

Both options are equally cost-effective

2. Project X costs $200K and generates $500K profit. Project Y costs $300K and generates $750K profit. Which has better ROI?

Solution:

ROI = (Profit - Cost) / Cost

Project X: ($500K - $200K)/$200K = 150%

Project Y: ($750K - $300K)/$300K = 150%

Both have identical ROI

3. Cleanup Method A costs $80/ton and removes 95% of plastic. Method B costs $65/ton but removes only 80%. Which provides better cost-per-effectiveness?

Solution:

Cost-per-effectiveness = Cost per ton / % removed

Method A: $80 / 95 = $0.8421 per % point

Method B: $65 / 80 = $0.8125 per % point

Method B is more cost-effective

4. A $500K marketing campaign attracts 8,000 new customers. A $300K campaign attracts 4,500. Which has lower cost-per-acquisition?

Solution:

Option A: $500K / 8,000 = $62.50 per customer

Option B: $300K / 4,500 = $66.67 per customer

Option A has lower CPA

5. Which project should you choose if you can only fund one?
Project X: $1M cost, $2.5M benefit
Project Y: $800K cost, $2M benefit

Solution:

Benefit-Cost Ratio = Total Benefit / Total Cost

Project X: $2.5M / $1M = 2.5

Project Y: $2M / $0.8M = 2.5

Both have identical BCR

6. A drone system costs $150K and can clean 1.2 tons/day. A boat system costs $90K and cleans 0.7 tons/day. Which has better cost-capacity ratio?

Solution:

Drone: $150K / 1.2 = $125K per ton/day

Boat: $90K / 0.7 = $128.57K per ton/day

Drone is slightly more efficient

7. Option A processes waste at $40/ton with 98% efficiency. Option B costs $30/ton at 90% efficiency. Effective cost per unit of actual waste processed?

Solution:

Effective Cost = Cost per ton / Efficiency

Option A: $40 / 0.98 = $40.82

Option B: $30 / 0.90 = $33.33

Option B is cheaper per actual ton processed

8. Which energy project is more efficient?
Solar: $2M capital, produces 400MWh/year
Wind: $1.8M capital, produces 350MWh/year

Solution:

Cost per MWh:

Solar: $2M / 400 = $5,000 per MWh/year

Wind: $1.8M / 350 = $5,143 per MWh/year

Solar is more efficient

9. A $75K AI system reduces sorting errors by 15%, saving $25K/year. A $50K system reduces errors by 10%, saving $18K/year. Which has faster payback?

Solution:

Payback Period = Investment / Annual Savings

$75K: $75K / $25K = 3 years

$50K: $50K / $18K ≈ 2.78 years

Cheaper system pays back faster

10. Compare two recycling programs:
Program A: $10/ton processing, 85% recovery rate
Program B: $14/ton processing, 92% recovery rate
Effective cost per ton actually recycled?

Solution:

Effective Cost = Cost per ton / Recovery rate

Program A: $10 / 0.85 ≈ $11.76

Program B: $14 / 0.92 ≈ $15.22

Program A is more cost-effective

Ecosystem Math

These problems test your ability to balance species populations in a food web. Key concepts:

  • Producer-Consumer Balance: Ensure enough producers (plants/algae) for herbivores
  • Predator-Prey Ratio: Maintain sustainable predator levels
  • Resilience Factors: Higher biodiversity = more stable ecosystems

McKinsey uses this to simulate environmental sustainability scenarios.

Ecosystem Math Drills

1. A lake has 500 algae (producers). Each small fish eats 10 algae, and each big fish eats 5 small fish. What's the maximum sustainable population of big fish?

Solution:

1. Max small fish: 500 algae / 10 = 50 small fish

2. Max big fish: 50 small fish / 5 = 10 big fish

2. A forest has 1,000kg of plants. Deer eat 5kg each, wolves eat 2 deer each. How many wolves can be supported?

Solution:

1. Max deer: 1,000kg / 5kg = 200 deer

2. Max wolves: 200 deer / 2 = 100 wolves

3. A coral reef has 200 coral polyps. Each parrotfish eats 5 polyps, each shark eats 3 parrotfish. What's the shark population limit?

Solution:

1. Max parrotfish: 200 polyps / 5 = 40 parrotfish

2. Max sharks: 40 / 3 ≈ 13.33 → 13 sharks

4. Select species to create a stable food chain with 8 organisms from:
- Algae (supports 100 fish)
- Plankton (supports 200 fish)
- Small fish (eat 10 algae or 20 plankton)
- Large fish (eat 5 small fish)
- Sharks (eat 2 large fish)

Solution:

Option B balances all levels:

- 20 small fish: 200 plankton / 20 = 10 plankton per fish (ok)

- 4 large fish: 20 small / 5 = 4 large fish

- 2 sharks: 4 large / 2 = 2 sharks

5. A meadow produces 800kg grass. Rabbits eat 2kg each, foxes eat 3 rabbits each. What's the maximum fox population?

Solution:

1. Max rabbits: 800kg / 2kg = 400 rabbits

2. Max foxes: 400 / 3 ≈ 133 foxes

6. You must select 8 species to stabilize a reef with:
- 300 coral (eaten by parrotfish)
- 400 algae (eaten by tangs)
- Parrotfish (eat 6 coral, max 50)
- Tangs (eat 8 algae, max 50)
- Groupers (eat 4 parrotfish or 5 tangs)

Solution:

Option B works:

- 40 parrotfish: 300 coral / 6 = 50 max (under limit)

- 40 tangs: 400 algae / 8 = 50 max (under limit)

- 10 groupers: 40/4 = 10 max (balanced)

7. A savanna has 1,000kg grass. Zebras eat 10kg each, lions eat 1 zebra every 5 days. How many lions can survive if zebras need to regrow?

Solution:

1. Max zebras: 1,000kg / 10kg = 100 zebras

2. Sustainable lion consumption: 100 zebras / 5 days = 20 lions

8. Select species to create a stable Arctic food web with 8 organisms from:
- Phytoplankton (supports 1M krill)
- Krill (eaten by 0.1% of population daily)
- Penguins (eat 1,000 krill/day)
- Seals (eat 5 penguins/day)
- Orcas (eat 1 seal every 3 days)

Solution:

Option A balances all levels:

- 1M krill can support 100 penguins (1,000 krill each)

- 100 penguins support 20 seals (5 penguins/day)

- 20 seals support ~6 orcas (1 seal every 3 days)

9. A wetland has 500 reeds. Ducks eat 2 reeds each, foxes eat 1 duck every 4 days. What's the maximum sustainable fox population?

Solution:

1. Max ducks: 500 reeds / 2 = 250 ducks

2. Fox consumption: 250 ducks / 4 days = 62.5 → 62 foxes

10. You must select 8 species for a stable forest ecosystem with:
- 1,000kg plants
- Deer eat 5kg each (max 100)
- Wolves eat 0.2 deer/day
- Bears eat 0.1 deer/day + 2kg plants

Solution:

Option A works:

- 80 deer: 1,000kg / 5kg = 200 max (under 100 limit)

- 10 wolves: 80 × 0.2 = 16 deer/day needed (ok)

- 10 bears: 80 × 0.1 + 10×2kg = 8 deer + 20kg plants (ok)

NPV Analysis

These problems evaluate long-term investments using time value of money. Key concepts:

  • Discounting: Future cash flows are worth less than present amounts
  • Formula: NPV = -Initial + Σ(Cash Flowt / (1+r)t)
  • Decision Rule: Accept projects with NPV > 0

McKinsey uses this for capital budgeting and sustainability investments.

NPV Drills

1. A $100K project generates $40K/year for 3 years. At 10% discount rate, what's the NPV?

Solution:

NPV = -100 + (40/1.10) + (40/1.10²) + (40/1.10³)

= -100 + 36.36 + 33.06 + 30.05

= -$0.53K ≈ -$152 (negative NPV)

2. A $1M solar farm saves $300K/year for 5 years. At 8% discount rate, what's the NPV?

Solution:

NPV = -1,000 + 300/1.08 + 300/1.08² + 300/1.08³ + 300/1.08⁴ + 300/1.08⁵

= -1,000 + 277.78 + 257.20 + 238.15 + 220.51 + 204.17

= $197.9K positive NPV

3. Calculate NPV for a $500K investment with these cash flows (r=12%):
Year 1: $150K
Year 2: $200K
Year 3: $250K

Solution:

NPV = -500 + 150/1.12 + 200/1.12² + 250/1.12³

= -500 + 133.93 + 159.44 + 177.95

= -500 + 471.32 = -$28.68K ≈ -$29K

4. A $2M wind turbine generates $500K/year for 6 years. At 10% discount rate, should you invest?

Solution:

NPV = -2,000 + 500 × (1 - 1/1.10⁶)/0.10

= -2,000 + 500 × 4.355 = $177.5K

Positive NPV → Invest

5. Compare two projects (r=8%):
Project A: $1M cost, $300K/year for 5 years
Project B: $800K cost, $250K/year for 5 years

Solution:

Project A NPV: -1,000 + 300 × 3.993 = $197.9K

Project B NPV: -800 + 250 × 3.993 = $198.3K

Project B has slightly higher NPV

6. A $5M factory upgrade saves $1.2M/year for 6 years. At 15% discount rate, what's the NPV?

Solution:

NPV = -5,000 + 1,200 × (1 - 1/1.15⁶)/0.15

= -5,000 + 1,200 × 3.784 = -$4.64K ≈ -$464K

Negative NPV

7. What discount rate makes NPV=0 for a $200K project with $60K/year for 5 years?

Solution:

Solve for IRR where 200 = 60 × (1 - 1/(1+r)⁵)/r

By trial/error or Excel: r ≈ 15.2%

8. A $1.5M investment has cash flows: $0 (Year 1), $500K (Year 2), $800K (Year 3), $1M (Year 4). At 12% discount rate, NPV?

Solution:

NPV = -1,500 + 0/1.12 + 500/1.12² + 800/1.12³ + 1,000/1.12⁴

= -1,500 + 0 + 398.60 + 569.42 + 635.52

= $103.54K ≈ $104K

9. A project has NPV=$150K at 10% discount rate. If the rate increases to 15%, will NPV be higher or lower?

Solution:

Higher discount rates reduce present value of future cash flows

NPV will be lower at 15% than at 10%

10. Calculate NPV for a $800K investment with $300K/year for 4 years, then $500K salvage value (r=10%)

Solution:

NPV = -800 + 300/1.10 + 300/1.10² + 300/1.10³ + (300+500)/1.10⁴

= -800 + 272.73 + 247.93 + 225.39 + 546.41

= $492.46K ≈ $492K
